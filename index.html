Gallery

Dive into the fascinating world of bacterial communities, meaning groups of bacteria that co-exist and grow together. They can be found almost everywhere: in the soil of fields, on plants and even in the human body. These microorganisms build complex relationships and can create astonishing structures when they colonise surfaces. The images shown here depict fluorescent bacteria growing in a culture dish. Their development, interactions and resulting patterns are followed by taking pictures with a microscope and analysed with informatics tools. In my thesis, I explore these relationships using synthetic communities, meaning simplified ones, made of genetically modified bacteria to fill in specific functions. I especially work on bacteria that help each other to grow, by exchanging either essential nutrients for their survival or genetic material for antibiotic resistance. These images are not just scientific illustrations; they reveal the hidden beauty of a microscopic world, inviting each and every one to appreciate the harmony and the complexity of bacterial relationships
